Maintaining a Healthy Sleep Schedule

The Importance of Sleep

As we all know, sleep is an important biological function necessary for life. When we fall asleep, many important functions take place which supports brain development, heart function, body metabolism, mindset, memory, emotions and so on. Sleep is especially important for children as it directly affects their growth and overall health which is why, on average, children needs more sleep than adults.

"Happiness in simplicity can be achieved with a flexible mindset and nine hours sleep each night" - Dalai Lama


Negative effects of sleep deprivation

Working from home can be a challenge especially for those who are not used to it. We are physically less active working from home, and we stare at a computer screen for an extended amount of time which can tire people out. To add on, sleep deprivation can cause fatigue, memory issues and decreased cognitive function which complicates our daily work and increases the risk of mistakes and accidents. Thus, maintaining a healthy sleep schedule is vital in ensuring work efficiency and sustain a healthy lifestyle

Statistics of our sleep

  • Adults need 7 or more hours of sleep per night
  • 50-70 million US adults have a sleep disorder
  • 100,000 deaths occur each year in US hospitals due to negligence where sleep deprivation have been shown to make a significant contribution
  • 37% of 20 to 30-year-olds report short sleep duration 
  • 40% of 40 to 59-year-olds  report short sleep duration 
  • 36% of the grown-up population in the US claim they feel drowsy and tired in the morning.
  • Sleep deprivation causes about 20% of all car crashes and trauma

Ways to manage sleep

There are a lot of factors that can interfere with a good night’s sleep, such as emotional state and illnesses. While you might not be able to control these, you can adopt these healthy habits that can aid with a better sleep.

Stick to a sleep schedule

Attempt to sleep and wake up at around the same time everyday. Maintaining consistency helps your body's sleep-wake cycle

Avoid these activities

We recommend avoiding stimulating activities before sleeping, such as video gaming, watching the television and social media

Creating a sleep-friendly environment

Ensure your room is dark, quiet and just the right temperature, as well as having a comfortable pillow and mattress

Avoid evening naps

Taking short naps during the day is alright, but napping near your usual sleeping time can make sleeping harder

Avoid these foods

Consuming alcohol, nicotine and caffeine affects your sleep quality. Also avoid sleeping while feeling stuffed or very hungry

Contact a doctor

If problems persist or you think something is seriously wrong with your sleep, then contacting a doctor is the best course of action
